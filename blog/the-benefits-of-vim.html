The benefits of VIM

If I was writing this a few months ago, it would be titled, "When to use VIM", as if it was not always worth using. This was before I realised I could use "VIM mode" in IDEs.

It is now "The benefits of VIM", as I now always use VIM in some form when writing code. In IDEs, I can use IDEAVim, a plugin which adds a "VIM Mode" to all IDEs made by Jetbrains.

Heck, I even use VIM in the browser .

I now have the powerful intelligence of IDEs, alongside the productivity-enhancing VIM.

But VIM didn't increase my productivity?

You didn't stick it out for long enough. VIM has a very steep learning curve, but once you learn it, and get a good .vimrc file to go with, it's great.

Yeah, your productivity will take a hit when you initially start learning - but you have to stay with it.

But why is it great?

I never have to use the mouse. Commands become ingrained into your muscle memory.

I no longer have to lose focus on the logic of my algorithm to navigate through a menu to find some text-editing function.

It's just in my head, in a command. If I don't know the command well enough such that I have to stop and think how to achieve something - then yes, I lose focus on the task. But if I have to do that function often enough, I will soon remember it and not have to lose focus.
