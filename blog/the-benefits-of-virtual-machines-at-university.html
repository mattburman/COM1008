The benefits of Free University VMs

The ability to play. The ability to fail. The ability to host.

Virtual machines allow students to learn all about operating systems. It would be great if we could just spin up a random Linux distro image, mess around with it, then destroy it as we please - all to learn.

The VMs would all be sandboxed, of course. They would not be allowed access to other machines on the network. They would be allowed public internet access though.

This would be free hosting for students - allowing us to host just because we can. Cost barriers put people off from tinkering.

Curious students would come out of University with Unix/Sysadmin experience, all for free - requiring no modules in the topic.

Sure - this would cost the University. I would personally argue it is a better investment than 12 NAO Robots, which are used irregularly, and haven't really taught me anything.
